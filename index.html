Adobo is a popular Filipino dish known for its flavorful and savory taste. It typically consists of meat (commonly chicken, pork, or a combination of both) marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns, then simmered until tender.

Sinigang is a traditional Filipino soup or stew characterized by its sour taste, which is typically achieved by using tamarind as a souring agent. It is often made with pork, beef, shrimp, fish, or chicken.

Kaldereta is a Filipino stew characterized by its rich and hearty flavor, typically made with meat the dish is often cooked with potatoes, carrots, bell peppers, and sometimes olives or green peas.

Bicol Express is a popular Filipino dish known for its spicy and flavorful taste. It originates from the Bicol region of the Philippines, hence the name. Primarily with pork, which is cooked in coconut milk and seasoned with shrimp paste and chili peppers.

Kare-Kare is a traditional Filipino stew known for its rich and flavorful peanut sauce. It typically consists of tender cuts of meat and a variety of vegetables simmered together in a thick, savory sauce made from ground roasted peanuts or peanut butter, with the addition of annatto seeds for color.

Fried chicken is a dish made by coating chicken pieces in flour or breadcrumbs and then deep-frying them until they are crispy and golden brown.

Pinoy Chicken Curry is a Filipino adaptation of the popular curry dish, incorporating local ingredients and flavors. It typically consists of tender pieces of chicken cooked in a flavorful curry sauce made with coconut milk, curry powder, and various spices.

Sisig is a popular Filipino dish that originated from the culinary traditions of the Pampanga province in the Philippines. It is typically made from parts of a pig's head and liver, which are first boiled, grilled, and then finely chopped. These chopped pieces are then seasoned with calamansi juice (or sometimes vinegar), soy sauce, and chili peppers for a tangy and spicy flavor.

Tokwang sisig is a variation of the traditional Filipino sisig dish that incorporates tofu (tokwa in Filipino) as the main ingredient instead of meat.
